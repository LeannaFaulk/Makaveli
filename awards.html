American Music Awards

The American Music Awards is an annual music awards show. Shakur has been nominated - first in 1994 then in 1997 - for Favorite R&B/Hip-Hop Artist. He won in 1997.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1994 Tupac Shakur Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop Artist Nominated
1997 Tupac Shakur Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop Artist Won

ASCAP Rhythm & Soul Music Awards

ASCAP is a membership association of more than 650,000 songwriters, composers and music publishers. We upload the value our members' music, and help them thrive alongside the businesses that used their music every day.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
2005 Runnin' (Dying to Live) (featuring The Notorious B.I.G) Top Soundtrack Song of the Year Won

Grammy Awards

The Grammy Awards is an accolade by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences of the United States to recognize outstanding achievement in the music industry. The annual awards ceremony features performances by prominent artists, and some of the awards of more popular interest are presente in a widely viewed televised ceremony. Shakur has been nominated 7 times.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1996 Dear Mama Best Rap Solo Performance Nominated
1996 Me Against the World Best Rap Album Nominated
1997 All Eyez on Me Best Rap Album Nominated
1997 "How Do U Want It" (featuring K-Ci & JoJo) Best Rap Solo Performance by a Duo or Group Nominated
1997 "California Love" (featuring Dr. Dre & Roger Troutman) Best Rap Solo Performance by a Duo or Group Nominated
2000 "Changes" Best Rap Solo Performance Nominated

MOBO Awards

The MOBO Awards are awards in "Music of Black Origin", established in 1996 by Kenya King and Andy Rufell. It is held annually in the United Kingdom to recognize artists of any ethnicity or nationally performing black music. Shakur was nominated and won for Best Video in 1996 for California Love.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1996 California Love Best Video Won

MTV Video Music Awards

The MTV Video Music Award or abbreviated as the VMA, is an award presented by the cable channel MTV to honor the best in music videos. Shakur has been nominated 4 times - first being in 1996 and lastly in 2003 - for Best Rap Video.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1996 California (featuring Dr. Dre and Roger Troutman) Best Rap Video Nominated
1999 Changes Best Rap Video Nominated
1999 Changes Best Editing in a Video Nominated
2003 Thugz Mansion (featuring Nas) Best Rap Video Nominated

NAACP Image Awards

The NAACP Image Award is an accolade presented by the American National Association for the Advancement of Colored People to honor outstanding people of color in film, television, music and literature. Shakur has been nominated only 1 time - first being in 1994.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1994 Tupac Shakur in Poetic Justice Outstanding Actor in a Motion Picture Nominated

Soul Train Awards

The Soul Train Awards is an annual award show which honors the best in Black music and entertainment. Shakur has been nominated first in 1996 for Best Rap Album and in 1997 for R&B/Soul or Rap Album of the Year and Best R&B/Soul or Rap Music Video. He was won 2 awards out of 3 nominations.

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
1996 Me Against the World Best Rap Album Won
1996 All Eyez on Me R&B/Soul or Rap Album of the Year Won
1997 How Do You Want It/California Love BestR&B/Soul or Rap Music Video Nominated

Source Awards

The Source Awards is an annual award show created by The Source Magazine which honors both hip-hop and R&B performers for their contributions to hip-hop. Shakur has been nominated in 2003 for Single of the Year (Male Solo Artist).

Year Nominee/Work Award Result
2003 Thugz Mansion (featuring Anthony Hamilton) Single of the Year Nominated
