Official Biography of Tupac Shakur

"Keep your head up, try to keep the faith and pray for better days"

Harlem, New York

Tupac Amaru Shakur, born Lesane Parish Crooks (June 16, 1971-September 13, 1996), was an American rapper and actor in the East Harlem section of Manhattan in New York City. In 1972, he was renamed TÃºpac Amaru II, after the 18th-century Peruvian revolutionary who was executed after leading an indigenous uprising against Spanish rule.

His mother, Afeni Shakur, and his father, Billy Garland, were active members of the Black Panther Party in New York in the late 1960s and early 1970s.

Baltimore, Maryland

In 1986, his family moved to Baltimore, Maryland. After completing his second year at Paul Laurence Dunbar High School, he transferred to the Baltimore School for the Arts. There he studied acting, poetry, jazz, and ballet. He performed in Shakespeare plays and in the role of the Mouse King in the ballet, The Nutcraker . He was remembered as one of the most popular kids in his school because of his sense of humor, superious rapping skills, and ability to mix with all crowds.

Shakur developed a close friendship with Jada Pinkett (later Jada Pinkett-Smith) that lasted until his death. A poem written by Shakur titled "Jada" appears in his book,The Rose That Grew from Concrete , which also includes a poem dedicated to Pinkett Smith called "The Tears in Cupid's Eyes."

Marin City, California

In 1988, Shakur and his family moved to Marin City, California, a suburban community located north of San Francisco. He attended Tamalpais High School in nearby Mill Valley. Shakur contributed to the school's drama department by performing in several productions. He began attending the poetry classes of Leila Steinberg in 199. That same year, Steinberg organized a concert with a former group of Shakur's,"Strictly Dope"; the concert let to his being signed with Atron Gregory. He set him up as a roadie and backup dancer with the hip hop group Digital Underground in 1990.

Shakur was friends with Mike Tyson, Chuck D, Marlon Wayans, Jim Carrey, and Rosie Perez. He befriended fellow rappers Snoop Dogg and Freddie Foxxx, collaborating on songs with the pair and wriing to Foxxx while in prison.

He had a three-year relationship with Madonna but ended things because she was white. In a letter explaining his decision he wrote, "I felt due to my "image" I would be letting down half of the people who made me what I thought I was." He was engaged to Kidada Jones at the time of his death in 1996.
