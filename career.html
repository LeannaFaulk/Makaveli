1987-93: Rise to Fame

Before using his first name as his rap name, Shakur went by the alias MC New York when starting his career in Baltimore. His professional entertainment career did not take off until the early 1990s when he debuted in Digital Underground's "Same Song." Shakur appeared in the accompanying music video. Shakur went on to feature Shock G and Money-B from Digital Undergroud in his track "I Get Around", which ranked #11 on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100.

In November 1991, Shakur released his debut studio solo album, 2Pacalypse Now. Though the album did not generae any "Top Ten" hits, 2Pacalypse Now is hailed by many critics and fans for its underground feel. 2Pacalypse Now generated significant controversy for numerous reasons. The songs "Trapped" and "Brenda's Got a Baby" were widely noted for their poetic qualities but also offered strong critiques of unjust social policies.

His second studio album, Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z... , was released February 1993. The album did better than its predecessor both critically and commercially, debuting at number 24 on the Billboard 200. It spawned the hits "Keep Ya Head Up" and "I Get Around" and reached platinum status. It is Shakur's tenth-biggest selling album, with 1,366,000 units moved as of 2004.

1993-95: Transition to the Big Screen

In late 1993, Shakur formed the group Thug Life with a number of his friends, including Big Syke, Macadoshis, his stepbrother Mopreme Shakur, and the Rated R. The group released their only album Thug Life: Volume 1 on Septemer 26, 1994, which went gold. As a result of criticism of gangsta rap at the time, the original version of the album was scrapped and re-recorded with many of the original songs being left out.

In addition to his endeavors in the music industry, Shakur acted in films. He made his first film appearance in Nothing but Trouble (1991) , as part of a cameo by the Digital Underground. His first starring role was int he 1992 film Juice . He then went on to star in Poetic Justice (1993) opposite Janet Jackson and the basketball drama Above the Rim (1994) . Following his death, three more completed films featuring Shakur were released: Bullet (1996) , Gridlock'd (1997) and Gang Related (1997) .

1995-96: Final Recordings

All Eyez on Me was the fourth studio album by 2Pac, released on February 13, 1996 by Death Row Records and Interscope Records. It was certifid 5x Platinum after just 2 month in April 1996 and 9x platinum in 1998. The album featured the Billboard Hot 100 number one singles "How Do U Want It" and "California Love."

Makaveli - The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory , commonly shortened to The 7 Day Theory , Shakur's fifth and final studio album and was released his new stage name Makaveli. The album was completely finished in a total of seven days during the month of August 1996. The album generated the second highest debut-week sales total of any album that year. It was certified 4x Platinum on June 15, 1999.
